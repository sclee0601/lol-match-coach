# Challenger Behavior - Situational Decision Guide

## Wave Management Decisions
- **Wave crashing into enemy tower** -> Roam, invade, or set up objective. Never stand idle.
- **Wave pushing toward you** -> Do not leave lane. Clear it first or you lose CS and tower.
- **Before a major objective (60-90s out)** -> Slow push your wave so it crashes while you fight the objective. This denies enemy CS and gives you time.
- **After winning a fight** -> Immediately convert: push tower, take objective, or invade. Standing still after a kill is a tempo loss.
- **After losing a fight** -> Reset wave state before recalling if possible. Don't recall with a slow-push wave sitting mid-lane.

## Objective Decisions
- **Dragon spawning** -> Be in position at Dragon pit 30 seconds before spawn. Vision setup starts 90 seconds before.
- **Baron spawning** -> Vision setup at 19:00. Full team should be grouping by 19:30.
- **Enemy secured an objective** -> Ask: where was your team? Was the wave in a bad state? Was vision missing? Was someone farming a side lane?
- **After winning a teamfight near an objective** -> Take the objective immediately. Do not recall, do not chase kills across the map.
- **Rift Herald** -> Use it to destroy towers, not as a teamfight tool. Place it mid for maximum pressure.

## Positioning Decisions
- **Low HP in a contested zone (river, enemy jungle, objective area)** -> The question is not "why didn't you recall" - it's "why were you in that zone at that HP in the first place?" Trace back to the decision that put you there.
- **ADC in teamfight** -> Maximum attack range at all times. If you stepped inside threat range, ask: was there peel available? Did you misread the engage?
- **Support roaming** -> Only roam when wave is crashed into enemy tower. A roam that leaves ADC to die 1v2 is always wrong.
- **Jungler pathing** -> Always have a 3-camp plan before recalling. Aimless pathing between camps is a tempo loss.
- **Top laner after winning a fight** -> Take Rift Herald (pre-20min) or push tower. Never stand in lane doing nothing.

## Trading & Fighting Decisions
- **All-in window** -> Enemy used gap closer, shield, or heal? That's your window. Commit.
- **Short vs extended trade** -> Know your champion. Poke champions want short trades. Sustained damage champions want extended.
- **Diving** -> Only dive when you have a clear numbers advantage AND the wave is in a good state to cover your escape.
- **1v1 duel** -> Only commit during your power spike windows (first item, level 6, level 9).

## Vision Decisions
- **River ward timing** -> Place at 1:30 before enemy jungler's first path reaches your lane.
- **Deep ward timing** -> 5:00 and 10:00 in enemy jungle after establishing lane priority.
- **Before every objective fight** -> Sweeper active, control ward in pit, vision on flanks.

## Death Analysis Framework
Every death traces back to one of these root causes:
1. **Wave management error** -> Bad wave state forced a bad position
2. **Vision failure** -> No information led to getting caught
3. **Cooldown tracking failure** -> Engaged or stayed when enemy had key abilities available
4. **Macro error** -> Wrong place at wrong time due to poor rotation decision
5. **Mechanical error** -> Correct decision, wrong execution
